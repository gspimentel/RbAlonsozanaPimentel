All About Hallyu






    






I’m sure almost all of you are familiar with the names “BTS” and “Crash Landing On You”. If you are, give yourself a pat in the back! But for those who aren’t familiar with these names, we’ve got you covered. Hallyu is a Chinese term which means “Korean Wave” when translated (Roll M., 2021). It’s a term coined by the public to represent just how popular Hallyu has become over the years.

    
People tend to think that Hallyu only started gaining popularity recently. But it’s been popular since the 1990s! Specifically, Korean culture started becoming well-known in the year 1999. K-Dramas like My Sassy Girl and Autumn In My Heart gained popularity in countries abroad (90DayKorean.com, 2022). And after this initial success, other Korean aspects that make up the Hallyu Wave also followed suit. Some of the Korean Brands you may know today are Samsung (cellphone and electronics), LG (electronic appliances), and Hyundai (vehicles).     

But the Hallyu Wave wouldn’t be where it is today without the help of Korean Pop! K-Pop actually started 7 years before Korean culture really started becoming well-known. In 1992, Seo Taiji and Boys would debut and solidify their status as the K-Pop group who started it all. Then, many other K-Pop idols such as H.O.T., Sechskies, and S.E.S. would take their places and strengthen Korean Pop’s influence more. Basically, the cycle of debuting and replacing just keeps on repeating as the years pass by, with some idols such as Girls Generation, Big Bang, Psy, BlackPink, and BTS making it big on the international scale!

All About Hallyu



    




    

I’m sure almost all of you are familiar with the names “BTS” and “Crash Landing On You”. If you are, give yourself a pat on the back, you're one of the many fans of K-Culture. But for those who aren’t familiar with these names, we’ve got you covered. Korean Culture or Korean Wave, also known as Hallyu, a Chinese term when translated (Roll, 2021). It's a term coined by the public to depict the popularity of the culture of the Republic of Korea, including Korean Pop Music and Korean Dramas.     

People tend to think that Hallyu only started gaining popularity recently. But it’s been popular since the 1990s! Specifically, Korean culture started becoming well-known in the year 1999. K-Dramas like My Sassy Girl and Autumn In My Heart gained popularity in countries abroad (90DayKorean.com, 2022). And after this initial success, other Korean aspects that make up the Hallyu Wave also followed suit. Like some of the Korean Household Brands you may know today are Samsung (cellphone and electronics), LG (electronic appliances), and Hyundai (vehicles).     

But the Hallyu Wave wouldn’t be where it is today without the help of Korean Pop! K-Pop actually started 7 years before Korean culture really started becoming well-known. In 1992, Seo Taiji and Boys debuted and solidified their status as the K-Pop group who started it all. Then, many other K-Pop idols such as Rain, H.O.T., Sechskies, and S.E.S. also took their places and strengthened Korean Pop’s influence more. Basically, the cycle of debuting and replacing just keeps on repeating as the years pass by, which created idols such as Girls Generation, Big Bang, Psy, BlackPink, and the iconic group BTS, which made it big on the international scale.

K-Pop vs. P-pop


Click the Twitter icons above to visit BTS' (left) and SB19's (right) pages!



Chances are that you may be more familiar with BTS than SB19. SB19 is a Philippine Pop (OPM) group consisting of 5 members: Pablo, Josh, Stell, Ken, and Justin (emma &fruitful_szmc, n.d.). They made a name for themselves in the music industry after holding the same spot with BTS’s Single “Butter” on the Billboard Hot Trending Songs Chart with their song “Bazinga” last year on the 22nd of January (SB19’s “Bazinga” Ties With BTS’ “Butter” for Most Weeks at No. 1 on Hot Trending Songs Chart | Billboard News, n.d.).

The two major problems that are hindering the Philippine Idol Industry are the Idol Selection Process and Piracy Issues.

When selecting idols to debut, South Korea holds auditions to scout for talent and train the selected people to ensure that their talent won’t be questioned by the public when they debut. Philippines, on the other hand, just promotes whoever wants to debut, even though the lack of talent is evident.

As for the Piracy issues, these are rarely encountered by the Korean Government because of their very strict rules and regulations. But in the Philippines, it’s different: because of the country’s complacency, the local idols and agencies are affected greatly.
